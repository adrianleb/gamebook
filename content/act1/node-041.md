## Node 041: Direct Confrontation

*The Understage — The Crossing Point*

You square your shoulders and face the threshold. This is what you are—or what you're becoming. A Prompter. Someone who stands between worlds. You refuse to slink into the Understage like an intruder or a supplicant.

You walk forward.

The moment you step across the boundary, the quality of existence changes. The air has weight. Sounds arrive from multiple directions, each carrying the echo of different scenes playing out somewhere in the endless dark. And you feel it—the Understage's attention, vast and narrative-hungry, turning toward you.

*Something* notices.

From the shadows between scenes, shapes gather. Not characters—not yet—but the raw material of stories. Archetypes waiting for forms. They press against the edge of visibility, assessing.

A voice emerges from everywhere and nowhere. It has the cadence of a hundred narrators speaking in unison.

"A Prompter claims passage. Bold. The last one who walked in so openly was Maren. And that was forty years ago."

The shadows ripple. You sense you're being tested.

"The Understage respects strength. But strength must be proven."

**[STAT CHECK: Stage Presence 3]**

---

**Success:** You meet the collective gaze without flinching. You've faced audiences before—literal or metaphorical—and you know the trick of it. Don't perform for them. Perform *at* them. Make them react to you instead of the other way around.

"I am the Prompter's apprentice," you say, and your voice carries with that strange theatrical resonance the Understage seems to amplify. "I enter on my own terms. I don't ask permission—I *give* it."

A pause. Then something that might be laughter, if laughter could be made of turning pages and closing curtains.

"Noted. The Prompter walks where they will. The Understage... watches with interest."

The shadows part. Before you, a path forms—not quite a corridor, not quite a stage. Something in between. A way forward that wasn't there before.

Behind you, Maren's voice, quiet but carrying: "Well done."

You've entered the Understage with your authority established. The characters here will respect you—or at least think twice before challenging you.

**Failure:** You try to hold the stare, but the weight of a thousand watching narratives is too much. Your eyes drop. Just for a moment—but the Understage sees everything.

"Ah," the voice says, and there's disappointment in it. Or perhaps satisfaction—predators enjoy prey that shows weakness. "The Prompter *claims* authority. But claiming is not the same as having."

The shadows don't part. They press closer.

"You may pass. You've earned that much by trying. But know this: you've entered as a threat that failed to threaten. The Understage will remember. And characters talk."

A path forms—the same path, the same way forward. But the quality of it is different. Darker. More watchful.

From behind you, Maren's voice: "It's not a failure. You're through. That's what matters." But you can hear what she doesn't say: it could have gone better.

You've entered the Understage, but your reputation precedes you—and not in the way you hoped.
