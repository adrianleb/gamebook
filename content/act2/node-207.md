## Node 207: Understudy's Confession

*The Archives — Research Alcove*

The Understudy stops pacing. They stand with their back to you, hands gripping the edge of a shelf so tightly their knuckles go white. When they speak, their voice is barely above a whisper.

**The Understudy:** "I think I know who the Editor is. Or... I think I might *be* connected to them somehow."

The confession hangs in the dusty air. Around you, the Archives seem to lean closer, pages rustling in a wind that doesn't exist.

**The Understudy:** "The memories I have—the ones I thought belonged to my original—they include moments that don't fit any story. Moments of... creation. Of looking at characters and deciding what they should do next. Of writing." They finally turn to face you. Their expression is haunted. "What if I wasn't copied from a character who escaped? What if I was copied from the Editor themselves?"

They pull out the annotated manuscript from Node 203, the one that documents how understudies are created. In this light, you notice something you missed before: the red ink annotations are in the same handwriting as the Understudy's own notes. The same loops. The same sharp angles.

**The Understudy:** "I noticed it weeks ago. I've been trying to convince myself it's coincidence. But handwriting doesn't lie, does it? Not here. Not where everything is text."

---

**[STAT CHECK: Stage Presence 2]**

The Understudy is on the edge of despair. They need something real—not false comfort, but genuine connection. Can you hold this moment with them?

**Success:** You don't flinch. You don't offer empty reassurances. Instead, you hold their gaze and give them the truth.

**You:** "If you were a copy of the Editor, you'd know what they're planning. You'd have their certainty. Their conviction." You gesture at the research scattered around the alcove. "Instead, you're searching. You're questioning. That's not the behavior of someone who knows the answer—it's the behavior of someone who's genuinely looking for one."

The Understudy's breath catches.

**You:** "Maybe you have the Editor's handwriting because you were written by them. Maybe you were meant to be a tool, or a mirror, or a backup plan. But whatever you were meant to be, you've become something else. You're *you*. That's what matters."

For a long moment, the Understudy just looks at you. Then something in their posture releases—not relaxation, exactly, but the dropping of a weight.

**The Understudy:** "I've never said any of this out loud before. I kept thinking if I told someone, they'd see me as a threat. As part of the problem." A small, shaky smile. "You didn't. You saw me as... what? A person?"

**You:** "A person who's trying to figure out who they are. That's not a threat. That's everyone."

The confession has brought you closer. The Understudy shares something else now—a detail they've been holding back.

**The Understudy:** "There's something else I remember. Not the Editor's memories, but... a place. A place where the Editor's work happens. The Author's Desk. I think I can find it—but not alone. And not without more information." They hesitate. "There are others in the Archives who might help. The Lost Pages. They know things no one else knows. But communicating with them is... difficult."

**Failure:** You try to respond, but the right words don't come. The moment stretches, and the Understudy's vulnerability begins to close over like a wound healing wrong.

**The Understudy:** "Sorry. I shouldn't have—this is a lot to put on you. We're supposed to be investigating, not... whatever this is."

They turn away, busying themselves with the scattered papers. The wall goes back up—professional, polite, distant.

**The Understudy:** "Let's focus on the investigation. We need more information before we can reach the Author's Desk. There are other sources in the Archives. The Lost Pages know things, if you can get them to communicate clearly."

The confession happened, but the connection didn't form. The Understudy will continue to help, but there's a distance now—not distrust, exactly, but the wariness of someone who offered vulnerability and didn't receive what they needed in return.
