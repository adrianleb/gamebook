## Ending Destinations

Five distinct endings reflecting different philosophical stances and accumulated choices:

### Ending 1: The Revised Draft
**Requirement:** High Revisionist alignment, Editor defeated
**Outcome:** You take the Editor's power and choose which stories survive and which are rewritten. Reality and the Understage remain separate, but you bear the burden of being the new Editor. Bittersweet; power with responsibility.

### Ending 2: The Open Book
**Requirement:** High Exiter alignment, Editor persuaded
**Outcome:** The boundary dissolves peacefully. Fictional characters can live in reality; real people can enter stories. Chaos, but free chaos. Hopeful but uncertain.

### Ending 3: The Closed Canon
**Requirement:** High Preservationist alignment, Editor defeated
**Outcome:** The Understage is sealed completely. Stories become static. Reality is protected but diminishedâ€”imagination itself feels smaller. Safe but melancholic.

### Ending 4: The Blank Page
**Requirement:** Independent path, Editor's truth revealed
**Outcome:** You discover the Editor's plan was a response to a deeper threat. Together, you choose to end *both* the Understage and its threat, leaving reality with no more new storiesâ€”but no more dangers from old ones. Tragic but peaceful.

### Ending 5: The Eternal Rehearsal
**Requirement:** Failed or refused final choice
**Outcome:** The conflict continues indefinitely. You remain a Prompter, endlessly managing a crisis that never resolves. Not a failure state, but a choice to embrace the liminal life forever. Ambiguous.

## Branch Philosophy

- **Choices should matter:** Every branch should offer genuinely different experiences, not just different flavors of the same outcome
- **Convergence with memory:** Paths may converge, but the game should remember how you got there
- **No dead ends:** Every branch should lead somewhere meaningful; no "you die, restart" without earning it
- **Foreshadowing:** Early choices should set up later payoffs
